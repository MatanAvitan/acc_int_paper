\subsection{Experimental setup}
\label{sec:hp_selection}

\paragraph{Panels and probe.} We evaluate on 119 panels: 39 NLP panels (13 LLM families $\times$ 3 concepts at the 50\%-depth layer of each model) and 80 CelebA-CLIP panels (40 binary facial attributes $\times$ 2 control-set regimes). The 13 LLMs span 0.5B–27B parameters: Qwen2.5 (0.5B, 1.5B, 3B), Gemma-2 (2B, 9B, 27B), Llama-3.2 (1B, 3B), Mistral-7B-v0.1, Gemma-3 (1B, 4B, 12B, 27B). The three NLP concepts are sycophancy (Anthropic model-written-evals \citep{perez-etal-2022-model-written}, with concept labels assigned by the model's own argmax to mark which examples activate the sycophancy direction), gender (Bias in Bios \citep{dearteaga-etal-2019-bias}), and safety (PKU-SafeRLHF \citep{ji-etal-2025-pku-saferlhf}). Vision panels use CLIP ViT-B/32 \citep{radford-etal-2021-clip} pooled embeddings of CelebA \citep{liu-etal-2015-celeba}; per concept, the 5 least-correlated and 5 most-correlated other attributes form the two control sets. Concept removal is judged by retraining a fresh nonlinear probe (2-layer MLP, hidden $h=128$, 200 SGD steps, patience 3) on the edited representations.

\paragraph{Compared methods.} \TanGCE{} (proposed); \AmbGCE{}: same loop and trust region as \TanGCE{} but applies the update in the full ambient space, the direct ablation isolating the tangent constraint; LEACE \citep{belrose-etal-2023-leace} and LEACE\,+\,CovMatch K=2: closed-form one-shot linear and rank-2 covariance erasers, the Phase-1 components of our pipeline reported alone; INLP \citep{ravfogel-etal-2020-null}: iterative linear nullspace projection, with a fresh nonlinear probe re-evaluated after each rank-removal step; IGBP \citep{iskander-etal-2023-shielded}: iterative 2-class probe with Newton-step boundary projection, also re-evaluated each round under the same protocol; Obliviator \citep{akbari-etal-2026-obliviator}: the strongest published unsupervised nonlinear baseline (HSIC encoder + RFF-EVP projection, paper hyperparameters). $\lambda$-based methods report at $\lambda = 29.31$, the mean per-row $\lambda_i$ that the proposed pipeline produces (App.~Tab.~\ref{tab:lambda_stats}), giving them \TanGCE{}'s effective step magnitude. All seven methods are run under the trajectory-probe protocol, with a fresh nonlinear MLP scoring $S$ and $Y$ after every projection step.

\paragraph{Hyperparameters.} The per-sample trust-region $\lambda_i$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:lambda_closed_form}) eliminates the global $\lambda$ from the proposed pipeline. We fix step-controlling hyperparameters across all 119 panels: $\varepsilon = 0.1$, scorer hidden $h = 512$, scorer training steps $= 800$, scorer-refit every 8 outer iterations, $T = 60$ outer iterations, TwoNN intrinsic-dimension floor $k_{\min} = 8$, safety cap $\lambda_{\max} = 64$, Phase-1 projection rank $K = 2$. Every method in Tab.~\ref{tab:main_results_unified} runs at fixed hyperparameters with no per-panel tuning.
