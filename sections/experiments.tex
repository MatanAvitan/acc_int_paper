% Experimental setup — settings, methods, and hyperparameters
\subsection{Settings, methods, and hyperparameters}
\label{sec:hp_selection}

\paragraph{Settings and probe.} We evaluate on 119 settings: 39 NLP settings (13 LLM families $\times$ 3 concepts at the 50\%-depth layer of each model) and 80 CelebA-CLIP settings (40 binary facial attributes $\times$ 2 control-set regimes). The 13 LLMs span 0.5B–27B parameters: Qwen2.5 (0.5B, 1.5B, 3B), Gemma-2 (2B, 9B, 27B), Llama-3.2 (1B, 3B), Mistral-7B-v0.1, Gemma-3 (1B, 4B, 12B, 27B). The three NLP concepts are sycophancy (Anthropic model-written-evals \citep{perez-etal-2022-model-written}, with concept labels assigned by the model's own argmax to mark which examples activate the sycophancy direction), gender (Bias in Bios \citep{dearteaga-etal-2019-bias}), and safety (PKU-SafeRLHF \citep{ji-etal-2025-pku-saferlhf}). Vision settings use CLIP ViT-B/32 \citep{radford-etal-2021-clip} pooled embeddings of CelebA \citep{liu-etal-2015-celeba}; per concept, the 5 least-correlated and 5 most-correlated other attributes form the two control sets. Concept removal is judged by retraining a fresh nonlinear probe (2-layer MLP, hidden $h=128$, 200 SGD steps, patience 3) on the edited representations.

\paragraph{Compared methods.} The \TanGCE{} family contributes three rows: \TanGCE{} (manifold-aware iterative loop alone, isolating the contribution of the manifold projection); \TanGCEp{} (LEACE preprocessing + manifold loop); \TanGCEpp{} (LEACE + rank-2 CovMatch preprocessing + manifold loop, the headline pipeline; ``CovMatch'' is shorthand for the rank-2 covariance-asymmetry projection defined in §\ref{sec:moment_preproc}). \AmbGCEpp{}: same closed-form preprocessing, same loop and trust region as \TanGCEpp{} but applies the update in the full ambient space, the direct ablation isolating the manifold constraint. The same recipe is used on both NLP and Vision. LEACE \citep{belrose-etal-2023-leace} and LEACE\,+\,CovMatch: closed-form one-shot linear and rank-2 covariance erasers, the preprocessing stages of \TanGCEp{} / \TanGCEpp{} reported alone. INLP \citep{ravfogel-etal-2020-null}: iterative linear nullspace projection, with a fresh nonlinear probe re-evaluated after each rank-removal step. IGBP \citep{iskander-etal-2023-shielded}: iterative 2-class probe with Newton-step boundary projection, also re-evaluated each round under the same protocol. Obliviator \citep{akbari-etal-2025-obliviator}: the strongest published nonlinear baseline (HSIC encoder + RFF-EVP projection, paper hyperparameters); supervised on the target concept and unsupervised on the controls, the same regime as our method. $\lambda$-based methods report at $\lambda = 29.31$, the mean per-row $\lambda_i$ that \TanGCEpp{} produces (App.~Tab.~\ref{tab:lambda_stats}), giving them \TanGCEpp{}'s effective step magnitude. All methods are run under the trajectory-probe protocol, with a fresh nonlinear MLP scoring $S$ and $Y$ after every projection step.

\paragraph{CelebA control-set construction.} On Vision, each of the 40 CelebA attributes is in turn used as the erasure target; the controls for that target are 5 of the remaining 39 attributes, and we report two complementary regimes. Let $\mathbf{R}_{k\ell}$ be the empirical Pearson correlation between attribute labels $k$ and $\ell$ on the train split. For each target $k$, the \emph{5 least-correlated} controls are the bottom 5 of the remaining 39 by absolute correlation $|\mathbf{R}_{k\ell}|$ (low-entanglement regime, the standard surgicality choice in prior work), and the \emph{5 most-correlated} are the top 5 (high-entanglement regime, the surgicality stress test). For example, when erasing \emph{Male} the most-correlated controls are \emph{Wearing\_Lipstick} ($|r| \approx 0.80$), \emph{Heavy\_Makeup}, \emph{No\_Beard}, \emph{Attractive}, \emph{5\_o\_Clock\_Shadow} --- attributes the dataset labels as nearly redundant with gender. Together this yields 80 settings (40 targets $\times$ 2 regimes); the per-target listings of both regimes with their $|r|$ values are in App.~Tabs.~\ref{tab:celeba_controls_least}--\ref{tab:celeba_controls_most}, and the construction details (formula, edge cases) are in App.~\ref{sec:appendix_celeba_controls}.

\paragraph{CelebA evaluation protocol.} For each target $\times$ regime, we extract pooled CLIP ViT-B/32 image embeddings of CelebA on the official train/val/test splits, fit the eraser on train, and re-evaluate after every projection step on test. The fresh nonlinear MLP (above) measures target leakage $S$ on the target attribute. Control-task accuracy $Y$ is the mean over the regime's 5 controls of an independent fresh MLP trained on each control attribute; the control-damage signal is $\Delta Y = Y - Y_{\text{clean}}$ (negative when controls degrade). The four budgets $\Delta Y \in \{-1, -3, -5, -10\}$pp are applied to this mean drop, so a method is in budget on a setting when its average control accuracy across the 5 selected controls dropped by at most $B$ percentage points relative to the clean (un-erased) representation.

\paragraph{Hyperparameters.} The per-sample trust-region $\lambda_i$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:lambda_closed_form}) eliminates the global $\lambda$ from the proposed method. We fix step-controlling hyperparameters across all 119 settings: $\varepsilon = 0.1$, scorer hidden $h = 512$, scorer training steps $= 800$, scorer-refit every 8 outer iterations, $T = 60$ outer iterations, TwoNN intrinsic-dimension floor $k_{\min} = 8$, safety cap $\lambda_{\max} = 64$, Phase-1 projection rank $K = 2$. Every method in Tab.~\ref{tab:main_results_unified} runs at fixed hyperparameters with no per-setting tuning.
