\subsection{Headline: distance to majority-class chance under control-damage budgets}

For each panel and budget $B \in \{1,3,5,10\}$pp we extract the trajectory step closest to floor (smallest $|S - S_{\text{floor}}|$) subject to $|Y_{\text{clean}} - Y| \le B/100$ and report the signed $(S - S_{\text{floor}})$ in pp averaged across the 39 panels. The right sub-column of each block reports panels at floor ($|S{-}S_{\text{floor}}| \le 0.5$pp) over panels reaching the budget. Bold marks every method whose mean is within $0.05$pp of the column-best. INLP and IGBP run under the same trajectory-probe protocol: each round projects out one source-probe direction, and a fresh nonlinear MLP probe ($h{=}128$, 200 SGD, patience 3) re-evaluates $S$ and $Y$ on the post-projection state.

\TanGCE{} is the only method whose mean approaches floor at every budget: $+2.6 \to +0.4$pp from $\le 1$pp to $\le 10$pp. Obliviator plateaus at $+4.0$ then $+2.7$pp. \AmbGCE{} (same trust region, no tangent constraint) leaves $+7$ to $+10$pp and fails to reach the budget on $6$ to $15$ of $39$ panels. The two iterative source-probe baselines plateau strictly above the trust-region methods: IGBP at $+9.4$pp and INLP at $+13.6$pp at the loosest budget, with @floor counts ($4$--$8/39$) below LEACE\,+\,CovMatch's $16/39$. The pattern follows the geometric reading: cumulative linear nullspace projection (INLP) and Newton-step projection on a 2-class probe (IGBP) close the source axis but do not constrain the step to remain on the local manifold, so a fresh nonlinear probe still recovers a residual nonlinear pocket. LEACE alone leaves an $\approx 18$pp gap that closed-form rank-2 covariance matching halves. \TanGCE{} reaches floor on $26$--$33/39$ panels (depending on budget); the best baseline (Obliviator) reaches it on $13$--$17/39$. \subsection{Cross-modal generalization: CelebA dual control regimes}
\label{sec:celeba}

\paragraph{Setup.} We extract pooled CLIP ViT-B/32 image embeddings (768-dim) from each CelebA face image, using the official train/val/test splits, and apply the same fixed-$\varepsilon=0.1$ pipeline used for the NLP panels. CelebA provides 40 binary facial attributes; for each attribute as the erasure target, we form two control sets from the remaining 39 attributes: (i) the \textbf{5 least-correlated} attributes by absolute Pearson $r$ on the train split (low-entanglement regime, the standard surgicality choice in prior work) and (ii) the \textbf{5 most-correlated} attributes by absolute Pearson $r$ (high-entanglement regime, the surgicality stress test). Concretely, erasing \emph{Male} under the most-correlated regime requires preserving \emph{Wearing\_Lipstick}, \emph{Heavy\_Makeup}, \emph{No\_Beard}, \emph{Attractive}, \emph{5\_o\_Clock\_Shadow} ($|r| > 0.4$ with Male on the train split). This yields 80 panels (40 attributes $\times$ 2 regimes). The control-task accuracy reported in $\Delta Y$ is averaged across the 5 controls of the active regime; App.~\ref{sec:appendix_celeba_controls} lists every attribute's two control sets.

\input{tables/celeba_unified_summary}

Tab.~\ref{tab:celeba_unified_summary} reports the CelebA equivalent of Tab.~\ref{tab:per_panel_breakdown}: signed $(S - S_{\text{floor}})$ in pp averaged across attributes that reached the budget, with the @floor count over the same. Three patterns emerge.

\textbf{Low-entanglement regime} (least-correlated controls). \TanGCE{}, Obliviator, and \AmbGCE{} all reach floor on the great majority of attributes; the linear and source-probe baselines (LEACE, LEACE\,+\,CovMatch K=2, INLP, IGBP) leave a $+5$ to $+6$pp gap above floor at every budget. \TanGCE{} leads on the at-floor count at the tightest $\le 1$pp budget ($35/39$ vs.\ Obliv's $15/15$, since Obliv reaches only $15$ of $40$ panels in budget at $-1$pp). At the looser budgets the trust-region methods converge: the least-correlated regime is the easy slice.

\textbf{High-entanglement regime} (most-correlated controls). The methods separate cleanly. Obliviator's mean $S - S_{\text{floor}}$ stays at $0.0$ but only because it \emph{walks over} the entangled panels: it reaches the budget on $2/40$, $15/40$, $30/40$, $34/40$ panels at the four budgets. Its first trajectory step already exceeds the budget on the heavily entangled targets (\emph{Male}/most: every Obliv step in $[-21.1, -13.2]$pp; \emph{Heavy\_Makeup}, \emph{Wearing\_Lipstick}, \emph{Smiling} all walkovers), so it never lands at all. \AmbGCE{} is the most unstable trust-region method: only $1$ of $40$ attributes reaches the budget at $\le 1$pp, and on the panels it does land it sits $+2$ to $+7$pp above floor. INLP and IGBP plateau at $+5.3$ to $+7.0$pp on every budget regardless of regime: with no on-manifold constraint, projecting out one source-probe direction does not generalize across the entangled tangent axes that target and controls share. \TanGCE{}'s closed-form $\lambda_i$ keeps it admissible on $15$--$35$ of $40$ panels with mean $+0.4$ to $+2.6$pp. This is the empirical signature of the local-$\sigma$ preconditioner: when target and controls share the dominant tangent axes, weighting each direction by its local manifold extent is what permits an admissible step at all.

The full per-attribute breakdown across all 7 methods on each of the 40 attributes under each regime is in App.~Tab.~\ref{tab:celeba_per_panel_breakdown}.
