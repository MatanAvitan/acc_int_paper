\subsection{Headline: distance to majority-class chance under control-damage budgets}
\label{sec:multi_model}

Tab.~\ref{tab:main_results_unified} reports the headline number for each method at four control-damage budgets, anchored to the per-setting majority-class floor. The \TanGCE{} family contributes three rows --- \TanGCE{} (manifold loop only), \TanGCEp{} (LEACE + manifold), \TanGCEpp{} (LEACE + CovMatch + manifold) --- so the contribution of each closed-form preprocessing stage is visible alongside the headline. For each setting and budget $B \in \{1,3,5,10\}$pp we extract the trajectory step closest to floor (smallest $|S - S_{\text{floor}}|$) subject to $|Y_{\text{clean}} - Y| \le B/100$ and report the signed $(S - S_{\text{floor}})$ in pp averaged across the 39 settings. The right sub-column of each block reports settings driven to chance ($|S{-}S_{\text{floor}}| \le 0.5$pp) over settings reaching the budget. Bold marks every method whose mean is within $0.05$pp of the column-best. INLP and IGBP run under the same trajectory-probe protocol: each round projects out one source-probe direction, and a fresh nonlinear MLP probe ($h{=}128$, 200 SGD, patience 3) re-evaluates $S$ and $Y$ on the post-projection state.

\paragraph{How to read the @chance counts.} The denominator of the @chance sub-column is \emph{not} the full setting count: it is the number of settings on which the method produced at least one trajectory step inside the control-damage budget. A method that overshoots the budget on its very first step on some settings simply never appears in the running for those settings -- it neither contributes to the mean nor counts toward the @chance numerator. This matters most in the high-entanglement CelebA regime (§\ref{sec:celeba}), where a method can post a flattering mean of $+0.0$pp by qualifying on only the easy settings and being silently absent from the hard ones. We therefore read each cell as the pair (mean leakage among reachable settings, @chance / reachable settings), and treat a method as fully admissible only when its denominator is close to the full setting count.

\TanGCEpp{} is the only method whose mean approaches chance at every budget: $+2.6 \to +0.4$pp from $\le 1$pp to $\le 10$pp. Obliviator plateaus at $+4.0$ then $+2.7$pp. \AmbGCEpp{} (same closed-form preprocessing and trust region as \TanGCEpp{}, but no manifold constraint) leaves $+7$ to $+10$pp and fails to reach the budget on $6$ to $15$ of $39$ settings. The two iterative source-probe baselines plateau strictly above the trust-region methods: IGBP at $+9.4$pp and INLP at $+13.6$pp at the loosest budget, with @chance counts ($4$--$8/39$) below LEACE\,+\,CovMatch's $16/39$. The pattern follows the geometric reading: cumulative linear nullspace projection (INLP) and Newton-step projection on a 2-class probe (IGBP) close the source axis but do not constrain the step to remain on the local manifold, so a fresh nonlinear probe still recovers a residual nonlinear pocket. LEACE alone leaves an $\approx 18$pp gap that closed-form rank-2 covariance matching halves. The two ablation rows in the \TanGCE{} family pin down the contribution of each closed-form preprocessing stage: pure \TanGCE{} (no preprocessing) already drives mean leakage to $+1.9$pp at the loosest budget, but reaches @chance on only $14$--$17/39$ settings; \TanGCEp{} (LEACE preprocessing) lifts @chance to $17$--$25/39$; \TanGCEpp{} (LEACE + CovMatch) reaches chance on $26$--$33/39$, while the best baseline (Obliviator) reaches chance on $13$--$17/39$. The manifold loop alone is competitive on leakage; the closed-form preprocessing converts that into a much higher fraction of fully-erased settings. The full per-setting breakdown is in App.~Tab.~\ref{tab:per_panel_breakdown}.

\subsection{TanGCE complements every Phase-1 baseline}
\label{sec:tangce_on_baseline}

To isolate the marginal value of the manifold-aware Phase~2 (\TanGCE{}), we apply the canonical TanGCE recipe ($\varepsilon{=}0.1$, $h{=}512$, scorer warm-start, $\le 60$ outer iterations) on top of each baseline's converged representations and re-evaluate under the same $\Delta Y$ budget convention as Tab.~\ref{tab:main_results_unified} (control-damage measured against the original clean reps' $Y$). Tab.~\ref{tab:tangce_on_baseline} reports the result. \TanGCE{} reduces leakage on every Phase-1 baseline that lands in budget --- LEACE drops from $\sim$20pp to $<$1pp, INLP from $\sim$16pp to $\sim$2pp, IGBP from $\sim$11pp to $\sim$1pp --- without further degrading $Y$ (the post-TanGCE step is selected from the same in-budget slice as the Phase-1-alone reading). Obliviator is a special case: it overshoots $\Delta Y$ on every sycophancy and gender setting ($26/26$), so it is in budget only on the $13$ safety settings, where its encoder collapses $Y$ to the panel's $Y_{\text{floor}}$ and the leakage shows $0.0$pp as a vacuous artefact rather than a surgical erasure (marked $^\dagger$ throughout the row).

The \TanGCE{} row is the manifold loop on raw reps with no Phase 1 --- already competitive on leakage (mean $7.7$pp at $\le 5$pp), but with much higher reach instability than any LEACE-stacked variant. The pattern is consistent with the geometric reading: every Phase-1 step that closes a linear or second-moment leakage axis frees TanGCE's per-row trust region to attack the residual nonlinear pocket, and the manifold-aware step erases that pocket without escaping the in-budget Y slice.

\subsection{Cross-modal generalization: CelebA dual control regimes}
\label{sec:celeba}

\paragraph{Setup.} We extract pooled CLIP ViT-B/32 image embeddings (768-dim) from each CelebA face image, using the official train/val/test splits, and apply the same fixed-$\varepsilon=0.1$ method used for the NLP settings. CelebA provides 40 binary facial attributes; for each attribute as the erasure target, we form two control sets from the remaining 39 attributes: (i) the \textbf{5 least-correlated} attributes by absolute Pearson $r$ on the train split (low-entanglement regime, the standard surgicality choice in prior work) and (ii) the \textbf{5 most-correlated} attributes by absolute Pearson $r$ (high-entanglement regime, the surgicality stress test). Concretely, erasing \emph{Male} under the most-correlated regime requires preserving \emph{Wearing\_Lipstick}, \emph{Heavy\_Makeup}, \emph{No\_Beard}, \emph{Attractive}, \emph{5\_o\_Clock\_Shadow} ($|r| > 0.4$ with Male on the train split). This yields 80 settings (40 attributes $\times$ 2 regimes). The control-task accuracy reported in $\Delta Y$ is averaged across the 5 controls of the active regime; App.~\ref{sec:appendix_celeba_controls} lists every attribute's two control sets.

\input{tables/celeba_unified_summary}

Tab.~\ref{tab:celeba_unified_summary} reports the CelebA equivalent of Tab.~\ref{tab:per_panel_breakdown}: signed $(S - S_{\text{floor}})$ in pp averaged across attributes that reached the budget, with the @chance count over the same. Three patterns emerge.

\textbf{Low-entanglement regime} (least-correlated controls). \TanGCEpp{}, Obliviator, and \AmbGCEpp{} all reach chance on the great majority of attributes; the linear and source-probe baselines (LEACE, LEACE\,+\,CovMatch K=2, INLP, IGBP) leave a $+5$ to $+6$pp gap above chance at every budget. \TanGCEpp{} leads on the at-chance count at the tightest $\le 1$pp budget ($35/39$ vs.\ Obliv's $15/15$, since Obliv reaches only $15$ of $40$ settings in budget at $-1$pp). At the looser budgets the trust-region methods converge: the least-correlated regime is the easy slice.

\textbf{High-entanglement regime} (most-correlated controls). The methods separate cleanly. Obliviator's mean $S - S_{\text{floor}}$ stays at $0.0$ but only because it \emph{walks over} the entangled settings: it reaches the budget on $2/40$, $15/40$, $30/40$, $34/40$ settings at the four budgets. Its first trajectory step already exceeds the budget on the heavily entangled targets (\emph{Male}/most: every Obliv step in $[-21.1, -13.2]$pp; \emph{Heavy\_Makeup}, \emph{Wearing\_Lipstick}, \emph{Smiling} all walkovers), so it never lands at all. \AmbGCEpp{} is the most unstable trust-region method: only $1$ of $40$ attributes reaches the budget at $\le 1$pp, and on the settings it does land it sits $+2$ to $+7$pp above chance. INLP and IGBP plateau at $+5.3$ to $+7.0$pp on every budget regardless of regime: with no on-manifold constraint, projecting out one source-probe direction does not generalize across the entangled tangent axes that target and controls share. \TanGCEpp{} produces an in-budget step on $16$--$40$ of the $40$ attributes and lands at majority-class chance on $15$--$35$ of those, with mean $+0.4$ to $+2.6$pp above chance. This is the empirical signature of the local-$\sigma$ preconditioner: when target and controls share the dominant tangent axes, weighting each direction by its local manifold extent is what permits an admissible step at all.

\textbf{Pipeline note.} The CelebA \TanGCEpp{} runs use the same Phase 1 as the NLP headline (LEACE $+$ rank-2 CovMatch $K{=}2$); the matched ambient baseline \AmbGCEpp{} uses LEACE-only preprocessing.

The full per-attribute breakdown is in App.~Tab.~\ref{tab:celeba_per_panel_breakdown}.
