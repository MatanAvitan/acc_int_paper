\subsection{Headline: distance to majority-class chance under control-damage budgets}
\label{sec:multi_model}

Tab.~\ref{tab:main_results_unified} reports the headline number for each method at four control-damage budgets. For each panel and budget $B \in \{1, 3, 5, 10\}$pp we extract the trajectory step closest to the per-panel majority-class floor (smallest $|S - S_{\text{floor}}|$) subject to $|Y_{\text{clean}} - Y| \le B/100$. Reported is the signed $(S - S_{\text{floor}})$ in pp averaged across the 39 panels; $0$ corresponds to majority-class chance, lower in absolute value is better. The right sub-column of each block reports panels at floor ($|S - S_{\text{floor}}| \le 0.5$pp) over panels reaching the budget.

\TanGCE{} is the only method whose mean approaches the floor at every budget: $+2.6 \to +0.4$pp from $\le 1$pp to $\le 10$pp. Obliviator plateaus at $+4.0 \to +2.7$pp; \AmbGCE{}-vanilla — same trust region, no tangent constraint — leaves $+7$ to $+10$pp and fails to reach the budget on $6$ to $15$ of $39$ panels. The gain is largest on gender (Fig.~\ref{fig:per_model_bars_gender}), where the rank-2 covariance projection of Phase~1 is most informative because gender concept information loads on second-moment asymmetry that LEACE alone cannot reach. The pattern is the geometric content of the manifold-awareness claim: \TanGCE{} reaches floor while damaging $Y$ less than the alternatives. Phase~1 alone (LEACE\,+\,CovMatch K=2) and the iterative Phase~2 contribute structurally distinct erasure — closed-form moment matching exhausts what global affine projections can reach; the per-row tangent step erodes the residual nonlinear pocket. \subsection{Cross-modal generalization: CelebA dual control regimes}
\label{sec:celeba}

We re-run the same fixed-$\varepsilon=0.1$ pipeline on 80 CelebA-CLIP panels: 40 binary attributes $\times$ 2 control-set regimes (least-correlated vs.\ most-correlated 5 attributes, by training-split Pearson $|r|$). The most-correlated regime is the surgicality stress test: erasing \emph{Male} while preserving \emph{Wearing\_Lipstick}, \emph{Heavy\_Makeup}, \emph{No\_Beard}, \emph{Attractive}, \emph{5\_o\_Clock\_Shadow} (all $|r| > 0.4$ with Male) shares dominant axes between target and controls.

\input{tables/celeba_clip_correlated_controls}

Tab.~\ref{tab:celeba_correlated_controls} summarizes the lowest probe accuracy reached subject to mean $\Delta Y \ge -5$pp on each control set. \TanGCE{} matches Obliviator in the least-correlated regime and walks over it in the most-correlated regime: Obliviator's first trajectory step already exceeds the $-5$pp budget by $10\!-\!22$pp on heavily entangled attributes (\emph{Male}/most: every Obliv step in $[-21.1, -13.2]$pp), leaving no admissible operating point. \TanGCE{}'s closed-form $\lambda_i$ keeps it inside the budget on the same panels. This is the empirical signature of the local-$\sigma$ preconditioner: when the manifold is most anisotropic — the closely correlated controls share the dominant tangent axes with the target — weighting each direction by its local extent is what permits an admissible step at all.
