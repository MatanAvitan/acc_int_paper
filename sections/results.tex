\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

This draft reports only the paper runs that have finished at the time of writing: the sycophancy tangent-vs-ambient sweep at layers 4 and 8, the Bias in Bios tangent-vs-ambient sweep at layers 4 and 8, the layer-8 iterative safety sweep, and the completed geometry summaries for gender and sycophancy. The iterative layer-16/23 sycophancy and safety sweeps are still running and are excluded from the quantitative claims below.

\subsection{Sycophancy: Nonlinear Concept Erasure}
Across all completed layer/lambda pairs, \texttt{gce\_tangent} yields lower sycophancy leakage than \texttt{gce\_ambient}. At layer 8, leakage falls from 0.202 to 0.142 at $\lambda=0.25$ and from 0.231 to 0.105 at $\lambda=1.0$. The same qualitative pattern holds at layer 4, where the best completed tangent point reaches 0.127 leakage versus 0.168 for the best ambient point.

The completed sweep does not yet support the stronger claim that tangent erasure wins by moving less or by preserving more utility. In the finished layer-8 run at $\lambda=1.0$, tangent also produces a larger mean displacement (0.728 vs. 0.048) and a larger answer-matching drop (-20.4 vs. -17.0 points). The current sycophancy evidence therefore supports better concept removal, but not yet a better leakage-utility-displacement frontier.

\subsection{Safety: Surgicality and Helpfulness}
The completed iterative safety sweep currently covers layer 8. Within that finished block, iterative tangent erasure reduces safety leakage much more aggressively than iterative ambient erasure: at $\lambda=0.5$, leakage is 0.113 for tangent versus 0.272 for ambient, and at $\lambda=1.0$, leakage is 0.068 versus 0.252.

This stronger removal again comes with a cost. At $\lambda=1.0$, tangent yields a helpfulness delta of -0.060 compared with -0.021 for ambient, while its mean displacement is 2.68 versus 0.20. The completed safety evidence is therefore promising on leakage, but still mixed on surgicality. The layer-16 and layer-23 safety sweeps are still in flight and are needed before we make a stronger claim.

\subsection{Bias in Bios: Diagnostic Baseline}
Bias in Bios does not reverse the pattern. Tangent again reaches substantially lower gender leakage in the completed runs: at layer 8 and $\lambda=1.0$, leakage is 0.062 for tangent versus 0.221 for ambient; at layer 4, the best completed tangent point reaches 0.084 versus 0.213 for the best ambient point.

However, the lower-leakage tangent points also reduce the downstream control-task accuracy more and require larger displacement. At layer 8 and $\lambda=1.0$, the downstream control-task accuracy is 0.513 for tangent and 0.536 for ambient, with displacement 0.847 versus 0.156. The completed linear anchor therefore supports stronger removal, not yet a cleaner utility-preserving regime.

\subsection{Geometric Evidence and Off-Manifold Displacement}
The completed geometry summaries preserve one strong part of the original paper story. Sycophancy is much more nonlinear than gender in the finished diagnostic runs: its local intrinsic dimension is only 34--53 across layers 4, 8, and 12, compared with 365--461 for gender, but its mean local-vs-global angle is 76.9--82.6$^\circ$ rather than 51.8--55.8$^\circ$. Pairwise scorer agreement remains high for both concepts (0.818--0.882), which suggests that the local direction field is coherent even when the global direction is a poor summary.

This geometry contrast keeps sycophancy as the right primary nonlinear case for the paper. What it does not yet prove, on the basis of the completed paper runs alone, is that tangent erasure already converts that geometric advantage into lower-displacement or higher-utility operating points.
