\section{Introduction}

Effective concept erasure must remove the target concept and preserve the rest of the representation \citep{dearteaga-etal-2019-bias,avitan-etal-2025-practical}. The hard part is recoverability: even after a chosen probe is driven to chance, a freshly trained classifier on the same edited representations can still recover the protected attribute \citep{elazar-goldberg-2018-adversarial}. Linear erasure has effective closed-form solutions \citep{ravfogel-etal-2020-null,belrose-etal-2023-leace} but does not block nonlinear probes \citep{ravfogel-etal-2022-adversarial}; nonlinear erasure remains open.

Hidden states live in $\mathbb{R}^d$, but the representations of natural inputs occupy a much smaller local support. Unconstrained ambient updates have the freedom to push representations off this support, which distorts unrelated features while leaving the target concept recoverable. We propose a different design: estimate a local tangent approximation around each sample by SVD of its nearest-neighbor secants, and constrain every erasure step to lie inside this estimated tangent space. The local singular spectrum produced by the same SVD additionally tells us the manifold's extent along each tangent axis; we reweight the step by it, so each direction moves proportional to how far the local neighborhood actually reaches in that direction. A two-stage closed-form preprocessing (LEACE + rank-2 covariance-asymmetry projection) exhausts 1st- and 2nd-moment concept signal before the tangent loop runs.

The empirical signature of this design is surgicality: at every control-damage budget, the proposed pipeline reaches the per-panel majority-class probe-accuracy floor on more panels than the strongest published unsupervised baseline while damaging the control task less. We evaluate on 119 panels under fixed pipeline hyperparameters (no per-panel tuning): 13 language models $\times$ 3 concepts at 50\%-depth, and CelebA-CLIP $\times$ 40 attributes $\times$ 2 control-set regimes. Our contributions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \TanGCE{}, a closed-form per-row trust-region tangent-constrained erasure method with a local-$\sigma$ preconditioner that ties step magnitude in each direction to the manifold's local extent in that direction.
    \item A 119-panel evaluation under a single fixed-$\varepsilon$ protocol on which \TanGCE{} reaches the floor on $26$--$33/39$ NLP panels (vs.\ $22$--$26/39$ for Obliviator) while preserving control-task accuracy.
\end{enumerate}
