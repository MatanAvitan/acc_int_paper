\section{Discussion}

The completed paper suite already narrows the central claim. In every finished comparison, the tangent-based method removes more measured concept signal than its ambient counterpart. That pattern appears in the sycophancy sweeps, in the completed layer-8 safety sweep, and in the Bias in Bios diagnostic. What the current paper snapshot does not yet show is the cleaner second half of the original thesis in which tangent projection also achieves lower displacement or uniformly better utility retention. In the completed runs, tangent reaches lower leakage by taking larger steps, and its utility metrics are either slightly worse or mixed.

The geometry diagnostics still matter because they explain why sycophancy is the harder case. Sycophancy shows local-vs-global angles near $80^\circ$, while gender remains near $53^\circ$, so a single global erasure direction is a much worse summary for the primary nonlinear concept. That is consistent with tangent-based methods improving removal in both concepts while leaving sycophancy as the clearly more nonlinear case, but the finished results currently frame tangent as a stronger remover rather than a dominantly better Pareto point.

The appendix snapshot tables slightly sharpen the near-term agenda. In the broader repository reports (Tables~\ref{tab:repo_snapshot_sycophancy} and \ref{tab:repo_snapshot_safety}), the same removal advantage continues at layers 16 and 23 for both sycophancy and safety, although the utility metrics differ by domain and report. We do not elevate those rows to headline claims because they were not part of the paper-locked evidence set, but they strengthen the case for spending the next round of effort on matched-leakage comparisons and deeper-layer replications rather than on searching for a different primary mechanism.

Two implications follow. First, the paper should presently avoid claiming that off-manifold control is already proven to improve the full leakage-utility frontier; the matched-leakage analysis and broader replications are still needed for that claim. Second, the current evidence suggests that the next important question is not whether tangent projection changes the erasure direction at all, but under which layers and operating points that removal advantage can be converted into a cleaner utility tradeoff.

The matched layer-8 linear-family comparison clarifies one part of that tradeoff. LEACE is more surgical than tangent on this slice because it removes linearly decodable concept signal while taking a much smaller geometric step and preserving more downstream utility, especially on sycophancy where the answer-matching penalty is substantially smaller. That does not make LEACE a substitute for tangent on the main nonlinear claim: LEACE succeeds by solving the linear erasure problem cleanly, whereas tangent remains the stronger method when the target concept is evaluated with a fresh nonlinear probe. The current evidence therefore suggests a division of labor rather than a single winner: LEACE is the cleaner linear baseline, while tangent remains the more aggressive nonlinear remover.
