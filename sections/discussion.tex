\section{Discussion}

\paragraph{Two coupled mechanisms produce the surgicality gain.}
The headline pattern is consistent: the only method that matches or beats Obliviator on probe accuracy at every budget while damaging the control task less is the tangent-projected one (Tab.~\ref{tab:per_panel_breakdown}, Fig.~\ref{fig:per_model_bars_gender}). Two coupled mechanisms produce this pattern. The tangent constraint defines \emph{where} edits live: ambient updates carry components weakly aligned with the local representation support, and tangent projection removes them at first order. The local-$\sigma$ preconditioner defines \emph{how far} to move along each axis: each tangent direction is updated proportional to the manifold's local extent in that direction, the singular value $\sigma_{i,k}$ already produced by the secant SVD. Together they restrict every step to a fraction of the local manifold-supported neighborhood. The signature is most visible in the most-correlated CelebA regime, where the dominant tangent axes are shared between target and controls and the preconditioned step is the only one that lands inside the budget on every panel.

\paragraph{Why the gain is largest on gender.}
Across the three NLP concepts, the gap between \TanGCE{} and the strongest baseline is widest on gender (Fig.~\ref{fig:per_model_bars_gender}, Tab.~\ref{tab:per_panel_breakdown}). Gender concept information loads on second-moment asymmetry that LEACE alone cannot reach; the rank-2 covariance projection of Phase~1 already cuts the residual but leaves a $\sim 15$pp gap above floor that the iterative tangent loop closes. Sycophancy is the easy concept: Phase~1 alone is indistinguishable from the floor (App.~Tab.~\ref{tab:leace_vs_tangce_nlp}), so the iterative loop adds nothing. Safety sits between the two. The per-concept ordering is consistent across model families and scales (0.5B--27B parameters); the tangent advantage is therefore not architecture-specific but tracks the underlying nonlinear structure of the concept.

\paragraph{Connection to low-rank fine-tuning.}
The local low-rank subspace structure that the tangent estimator exploits has a parameter-space analogue. \citet{aghajanyan-etal-2021-intrinsic} show that fine-tuning objectives have intrinsic dimensionality of order $200$--$1{,}000$, and \citet{hu-etal-2022-lora} exploit this directly via low-rank weight updates. Our finding is the activation-space analogue of the same phenomenon: hidden-state directions that carry task-specific information occupy a much smaller local support than the full ambient space. The two are mechanistically linked --- if layer-$\ell$ activations have intrinsic rank $m$, then weight-space gradients at that layer are also rank-$m$ at the activation level, formed as outer products of the low-rank activations and the corresponding error signals. From this perspective, LoRA and tangent-constrained concept erasure both follow from the same underlying structure in large-model representations, and the operative distinction is whether the update is allowed to act freely in ambient space or is constrained to the manifold support that the representations actually occupy.
