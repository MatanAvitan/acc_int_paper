Let $p_{\mathrm{nat}}$ be a distribution over natural inputs $s \in \Sigma^*$, and let
$\mathrm{enc}_\ell : \Sigma^* \to \mathbb{R}^d$ be the layer-$\ell$ encoder of a language model.
For a random natural input $S \sim p_{\mathrm{nat}}$, define the layer-$\ell$ representation random vector
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{X}_\ell = \mathrm{enc}_\ell(S) \in \mathbb{R}^d .
\end{equation}
Let $p_\ell$ denote the distribution of $\mathbf{X}_\ell$ over representation space. We define the
\emph{natural representation support} as
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:natural_rep_support}
    \mathcal{S}_\ell
    =
    \mathrm{supp}(p_\ell)
    =
    \bigcap_{\substack{
    A \subseteq \mathbb{R}^d,\ A \text{ closed} \\
    \Pr(\mathbf{X}_\ell \in A)=1
    }} A .
\end{equation}
Informally, $\mathcal{S}_\ell$ is the region of representation space occupied by hidden states
produced by natural inputs.

For each observed sample $i$, we have a representation
$\mathbf{x}_i = \mathrm{enc}_\ell(s_i) \in \mathbb{R}^d$, a binary concept label
$y_i \in \{0,1\}$, and an optional control label $z_i$. The dataset is
$\mathcal{D} = \{(\mathbf{x}_i, y_i, z_i)\}_{i=1}^N$, with class subsets
$\mathcal{D}_c = \{i : y_i = c\}$. A concept scorer
$f_\theta : \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{R}$ is a 2-layer MLP with input gradient
$\nabla f_\theta(\mathbf{x}_i)$.

\paragraph{Local manifold hypothesis.}
Although $\mathbf{X}_\ell$ takes values in the ambient space $\mathbb{R}^d$, we do not expect
$p_\ell$ to occupy this space uniformly. We assume that, around observed natural representations,
the support $\mathcal{S}_\ell$ is locally well approximated by a low-dimensional manifold-like
structure $\mathcal{M}_\ell \subset \mathbb{R}^d$. The operational consequence is that the
neighborhood of a point $\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{S}_\ell$ can be approximated by a tangent space
$T_{\mathbf{x}}\mathcal{M}_\ell$ \citep{tu-2011-manifolds}, allowing us to apply local linear
operations such as SVD, projection, and anisotropic scaling.

The tangent space is not observed directly. We estimate it from nearby representations by local SVD
on nearest-neighbor secants (§\ref{sec:method}). For an ambient vector
$\mathbf{g} \in \mathbb{R}^d$, the tangent decomposition
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{g}
    =
    \mathbf{g}^{\top}
    +
    \mathbf{g}^{\perp},
    \qquad
    \mathbf{g}^{\top} \in T_{\mathbf{x}}\mathcal{M}_\ell,
    \qquad
    \mathbf{g}^{\perp} \perp T_{\mathbf{x}}\mathcal{M}_\ell,
\end{equation}
separates the component supported by the local representation geometry from the component
orthogonal to it. \TanGCE{} acts on the tangent component only; \AmbGCE{} retains both. Formal
coordinate-chart definitions and the differential $d(\varphi^{-1})_0$ identification with
$T_{\mathbf{x}}\mathcal{M}_\ell$ are deferred to App.~\ref{sec:appendix_geometry}.

\paragraph{Recoverability.}
Concept removal is judged only after training a \emph{fresh} probe on the edited representations
\citep{elazar-goldberg-2018-adversarial}: erasure succeeds if a newly trained nonlinear classifier
fails to recover the concept from $\tilde{\mathbf{x}}$, not merely if the old scorer is fooled.
