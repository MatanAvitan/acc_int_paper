\section{Method: TanGCE}
\label{sec:method}

TanGCE is motivated by a mismatch between ambient dimension and effective representation dimension. The hidden space of the model may be large, but the representations of actual sentences need not explore all of it; they can concentrate near a much lower-dimensional natural manifold. In that regime, an unrestricted gradient step is likely to contain a substantial off-manifold component. TanGCE addresses exactly this failure mode by restricting the erasure update to a local tangent approximation of the manifold.

We implement TanGCE using an iterative protocol with probe recycling. The rationale is simple: success against a single trained classifier is not evidence that the concept has been exhausted. Elazar and Goldberg \citep{elazar-goldberg-2018-adversarial} showed that even when an adversarial removal objective appears successful, a new post-hoc classifier can still recover the protected attribute. Iterative linear methods such as INLP \citep{ravfogel-etal-2020-null} and intervention-based analyses such as amnesic probing \citep{elazar-etal-2021-amnesic} build on the same insight. We adopt that logic directly in the nonlinear setting.

Each round of TanGCE begins by training a fresh concept scorer on the current (possibly already partially erased) representations, computing per-sample gradients, and applying the tangent-constrained erasure step (Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase}). This turns the method into an iterative nonlinear eraser: every new scorer is trained specifically to recover whatever target information survived the previous round. The tangent estimator is refit in every round to track the evolving local geometry as the representations shift.

This setup also clarifies the relationship between TanGCE, Ambient GCE, and IGBP. IGBP \citep{iskander-etal-2023-shielded} performs a one-shot correction to the current decision boundary in each round and serves as the closest prior nonlinear baseline. Ambient GCE is our ablation: it keeps the same fresh-scorer loop but replaces the exact boundary projection with a controllable $\lambda$-scaled gradient update in the ambient space. TanGCE is the full method, adding the manifold constraint by projecting that ambient update onto the local tangent space.

Two diagnostics verify tangent quality throughout the iterative process. \emph{Tangent Gradient Capture} (TGC) measures the fraction of the leakage gradient that lies within the estimated tangent space. \emph{Tangent Neighbor Residual} (TNR) measures the fraction of local secant vectors not explained by the tangent basis. High TGC and low TNR confirm that the tangent estimation remains geometrically sound.
