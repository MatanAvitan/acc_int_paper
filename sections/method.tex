\section{Method: \TanGCE{}}
\label{sec:method}

\TanGCE{} (\emph{Tangent Gradient-based Concept Erasure}) has two phases. Phase~1 applies two closed-form moment-matching projections that exhaust 1st- and 2nd-moment concept signal. Phase~2 iterates a tangent-constrained step on the residual: each round trains a fresh nonlinear scorer, projects its gradient onto a locally estimated tangent basis, and applies the projection-and-preconditioning update.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{\TanGCE{} at a glance. Phase~1: LEACE + rank-2 covariance-asymmetry projection. Phase~2: iterative tangent-constrained step.}
\label{alg:tangce}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\REQUIRE Representations $\mathbf{X}$, concept labels $\mathbf{y}$, $\#$rounds $T$, trust region $\varepsilon$, anisotropy $\alpha$, scorer hidden $h$.
\STATE \textbf{Phase~1 -- closed-form moment preprocessing} (Eqs.~\ref{eq:delta_sigma}--\ref{eq:covmatch_apply}):
\STATE \hspace{1em} \textbf{1a.} $\mathbf{X} \leftarrow \mathrm{LEACE}_{\mathbf{y}}(\mathbf{X})$ \hfill (rank-1 mean projection)
\STATE \hspace{1em} \textbf{1b.} $\mathbf{X} \leftarrow \mathbf{X} - \mathbf{D}\mathbf{D}^\top \mathbf{X}$, with $\mathbf{D}$ = top-2 eigvecs of $\Delta\mathbf{\Sigma}$ \hfill (rank-2 covariance projection)
\STATE \textbf{Phase~2 -- iterative tangent step} (Eqs.~\ref{eq:tangent_proj}--\ref{eq:tangent_erase_cov}):
\FOR{$t = 1, \ldots, T$}
 \STATE Train a fresh 2-layer MLP scorer $f_t$ (hidden $h$) on the current $(\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{y})$.
 \STATE \textbf{For each row $\mathbf{x}_i$}: tangent-projected, $\sigma$-preconditioned trust-region step
 \STATE \hspace{2em} $\displaystyle \mathbf{x}_i \;\leftarrow\; \mathbf{x}_i - \lambda_i \cdot \mathbf{C}_i^{1/2} \cdot \mathbf{P}_{T_i} \nabla f_t(\mathbf{x}_i), \quad \|\Delta\mathbf{x}_i\|_2 \leq \varepsilon \cdot s_i.$
\ENDFOR
\STATE \textbf{return} edited $\mathbf{X}$.
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Phase~1: closed-form moment preprocessing}
\label{sec:moment_preproc}

\paragraph{LEACE (1st moment).}
Fit a LEACE eraser \citep{belrose-etal-2023-leace} on $(\mathbf{X}_{\text{train}}, \mathbf{y})$ and apply to all splits. LEACE removes the rank-1 cross-covariance $\mathbf{\Sigma}_{xz}$, leaving no \emph{linear} classifier above majority-class accuracy with provably minimum representation damage among affine projections.

\paragraph{Covariance-asymmetry projection (2nd moment).}
LEACE matches class means but leaves the within-class asymmetry
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:delta_sigma}
\Delta\mathbf{\Sigma} = \mathbf{\Sigma}_{+} - \mathbf{\Sigma}_{-}.
\end{equation}
A nonlinear probe still recovers concept information from variance asymmetry along axes where $\mathbf{\Sigma}_{+}$ and $\mathbf{\Sigma}_{-}$ differ. We append a rank-$2$ projection: the top-$2$ eigenvectors of $\Delta\mathbf{\Sigma}$ ranked by $|\lambda|$, orthonormalized with a residual mean direction via QR,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:covmatch_basis}
\mathbf{D} = \mathrm{QR}\!\left([\,\hat{\mathbf{d}}_{\text{mean}} \;\big|\; \mathbf{e}_1, \mathbf{e}_2\,]\right) \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times 3},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:covmatch_apply}
\tilde{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{x} - \mathbf{D}\mathbf{D}^\top \mathbf{x}.
\end{equation}
Because LEACE has aligned class means, $\hat{\mathbf{d}}_{\text{mean}} \approx \mathbf{0}$ and the effective rank removed is two. This is a special case of the $k$-LEACE framework of \citet{singh-ravfogel-2024-representation-surgery} restricted to second-order eigenvectors, which are stable to estimate from $n \approx 7000$ examples while higher-order tensors are not. We fix $K{=}2$ across all 119 panels: for a binary concept, the top-2 eigenvectors of $\Delta\mathbf{\Sigma}$ carry the two axes of class-conditional variance asymmetry; subsequent eigenvectors describe noise rather than class-discriminative signal. Empirically, $K{\ge}3$ adds no leakage reduction and begins to erode control accuracy (see App.~\ref{sec:appendix_per_panel}).

The two stages erase structurally distinct signal (mean shift vs.\ variance asymmetry) and together remove rank $\le 3$ from $d \in \{2048, 3072, 3584\}$, exposing the residual nonlinear structure that Phase~2 attacks.

\subsection{Local tangent estimation}

For each query $\mathbf{x}_i$, let $\mathcal{N}_k(\mathbf{x}_i)$ be its $k$ nearest neighbors. Stack the centered secants $\mathbf{s}_{j_m} = \mathbf{x}_{j_m} - \mathbf{x}_i$ row-wise into $\mathbf{S}_i \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times d}$ and take its SVD,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:secant_svd}
\mathbf{S}_i = \mathbf{U}_i \, \mathrm{diag}(\sigma_{i,1}, \ldots, \sigma_{i,k}) \, \mathbf{V}_i^\top.
\end{equation}
The right singular vectors $\mathbf{V}_i$ are the directions in which neighbors extend; the singular values $\sigma_{i,k}$ are how far they extend along each. The implied local covariance is $\mathbf{C}_i = \tfrac{1}{k}\mathbf{V}_i \, \mathrm{diag}(\sigma_{i,k}^2) \, \mathbf{V}_i^\top$. Truncating to the top $r$ right singular vectors gives the tangent basis
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_basis}
\mathbf{B}_i = [\mathbf{v}_i^{(1)}, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_i^{(r)}] \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times r},
\end{equation}
with $r$ estimated per panel by TwoNN \citep{facco-etal-2017-two-nn} and floored at $r \ge 8$. The tangent projector is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_proj}
\mathbf{P}_{T_i} = \mathbf{B}_i \mathbf{B}_i^\top.
\end{equation}
We treat the SVD basis as a first-order stand-in for $T_{\mathbf{x}_i}\mathcal{M}$, not as a guarantee of smooth-manifold structure.

\subsection{Tangent-constrained update}

The ambient unit gradient at $\mathbf{x}_i$ is $\mathbf{u}_i = \nabla f_t(\mathbf{x}_i) / \|\nabla f_t(\mathbf{x}_i)\|$. \TanGCE{} replaces it with its tangent projection,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_direction}
\mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}} = \mathbf{P}_{T_i} \mathbf{u}_i = \mathbf{B}_i (\mathbf{B}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_i),
\end{equation}
not re-normalized: $\|\mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}\| \le 1$ acts as an overlap weight that attenuates the update when the ambient gradient has little support in the estimated tangent space. The isotropic update is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_erase}
\tilde{\mathbf{x}}_i = \mathbf{x}_i - \lambda \, (\mathbf{x}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}) \, \mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}},
\end{equation}
with $\lambda$ a step-size scalar (replaced below by a closed-form per-sample $\lambda_i$). \AmbGCE{} is the direct ablation: same loop, same scorer, same $\lambda$, but applies the update in the full ambient space without the projection. We retain Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase} as the conceptual entry point and as the $\alpha = 0$ ablation; the canonical \TanGCE{} update is the $\sigma$-preconditioned generalization in §\ref{sec:cov_tangce}.

\subsection{Adaptive per-sample step size: closed-form \texorpdfstring{$\lambda_i$}{lambda}}
\label{sec:closed_form_lambda}

A single global $\lambda$ over-edits some samples while under-editing others. We replace it with a per-sample step size that respects a \emph{local trust region}: no sample moves more than a fraction $\varepsilon$ of a per-sample local scale $s_i$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:trust_region}
\| \tilde{\mathbf{x}}_i - \mathbf{x}_i \|_2 \le \varepsilon \cdot s_i.
\end{equation}
Substituting Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase} and letting $\mathbf{v}_i = (\mathbf{x}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}) \mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}$ be the per-sample displacement at $\lambda{=}1$, the largest admissible step is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:lambda_closed_form}
\boxed{\;\;\lambda_i = \min\!\left( \lambda_{\max}, \; \frac{\varepsilon \cdot s_i}{\|\mathbf{v}_i\|} \right)\;\;}
\end{equation}
with $\lambda_{\max}=64$ a safety cap when $\|\mathbf{v}_i\| \to 0$. We use $\varepsilon = 0.1$ across every panel.

\paragraph{Anisotropy-aware local scale (FMaHa).}
The local manifold is an ellipsoid: along high-$\sigma$ singular directions, neighbors extend much further than along thin tail directions. We therefore use the \emph{whitened-tangent neighborhood radius}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:s_fmaha}
s_i = R_i^{\mathrm{white}} \cdot s_i^{\mathrm{maha}}, \qquad
R_i^{\mathrm{white}} = \mathrm{median}_{j \in \mathcal{N}_k(i)} \, \|\mathbf{B}_i^\top (\mathbf{x}_j - \mathbf{x}_i) / \boldsymbol\sigma_i \|_2,
\end{equation}
with $\boldsymbol\sigma_i = (\sigma_{i,1}, \ldots, \sigma_{i,r})$ and $s_i^{\mathrm{maha}} = 1 / \|\boldsymbol\alpha_i / (\boldsymbol\sigma_i + \delta)\|_2$ (component-wise). $\varepsilon$ is then a fraction of the local whitened-tangent neighborhood; setting $s_i = r_i$ (mean $k$-NN distance) recovers the Euclidean radial scale and is reported in App.\ as an ablation.

\subsection{Anisotropic update via the local \texorpdfstring{$\sigma$}{sigma}-spectrum}
\label{sec:cov_tangce}

The closed-form $\lambda_i$ controls the magnitude of each step; its direction is still isotropic in the tangent basis. The local SVD already exposes anisotropy — the squared singular values $\sigma_{i,k}^2$ differ by orders of magnitude across $k$. An isotropic step over-edits thin tail directions where the linear approximation is fragile, and under-edits dominant directions where the manifold easily accommodates motion.

We replace Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase} with a covariance-preconditioned step,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_erase_cov}
\boxed{\;\;\tilde{\mathbf{x}}_i = \mathbf{x}_i - \lambda_i \, \mathbf{C}_i^{1/2} \, \mathbf{v}_i = \mathbf{x}_i - \lambda_i \sum_{k=1}^{r} \sigma_{i,k} \, a_{i,k} \, \mathbf{v}_i^{(k)}\;\;}
\end{equation}
where $a_{i,k} = \langle \mathbf{v}_i, \mathbf{v}_i^{(k)} \rangle$ are the SVD-basis coordinates of $\mathbf{v}_i$. Each tangent coefficient is reweighted by its singular value: the update moves more along high-$\sigma$ directions and less along thin ones.

\paragraph{Relation to Mahalanobis.} Mahalanobis distance normalizes by a \emph{global} covariance $\mathbf{\Sigma}^{-1/2}$. Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase_cov} normalizes by a \emph{local, per-direction} factor $\sigma_{i,k}$ instead. This choice is forced by the geometry: we have already decomposed the tangent step in the local SVD eigenvector coordinates $\{\mathbf{v}_i^{(k)}\}$, and the natural per-axis normalization in those coordinates is the corresponding eigenvalues. The general $\mathbf{C}_i^{\alpha}$ family is detailed in App.~\ref{sec:appendix_alpha_regimes}; we use $\alpha = \tfrac{1}{2}$ (positive half-power) throughout, never the whitening sign $\alpha = -\tfrac{1}{2}$.

The trust-region magnitude in Eq.~\ref{eq:lambda_closed_form} is enforced after preconditioning (replacing $\|\mathbf{v}_i\|$ by $\|\mathbf{C}_i^{1/2}\mathbf{v}_i\|$ in the denominator), so the displacement still respects $\varepsilon \cdot s_i$. Anisotropy reshapes the \emph{direction} of the step, not its budget.

\subsection{Iterative loop and relation to baselines}

Each round trains a fresh nonlinear scorer on the current $(\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{y})$, recomputes per-sample gradients, and applies the update. The fresh-scorer loop follows \citet{elazar-goldberg-2018-adversarial}: success against any single trained classifier is not evidence the concept is gone. \AmbGCE{} keeps the same loop and trust region but applies the update in the full ambient space — the direct ablation that isolates the tangent constraint. IGBP \citep{iskander-etal-2023-shielded} is the closest prior nonlinear baseline; Obliviator \citep{akbari-etal-2026-obliviator} is the strongest published unsupervised baseline.
