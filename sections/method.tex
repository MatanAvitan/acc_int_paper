\section{Method: \TanGCE{} family}
\label{sec:method}

\TanGCE{} (\emph{Tangent Gradient-based Concept Erasure}) operationalizes the Manifold Representation Hypothesis (§\ref{sec:mrh}) by constraining every erasure update to lie in the tangent space of $\mathcal{M}$ at the current point. At each round it trains a fresh nonlinear scorer on the current $(\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{y})$, projects the scorer's gradient onto a locally estimated tangent basis, and applies a spectrally-normalized trust-region update. The scorer (a 2-layer MLP with hidden width $h{=}512$, $800$ steps) is intentionally distinct from the smaller evaluation probe ($h{=}128$, 200 SGD steps, §\ref{sec:evaluation}), so that erasure quality is judged by an independently trained classifier rather than by the one that produced the update.

\paragraph{Local estimation of the manifold.}
A globally consistent estimate of $\mathcal{M}$ is intractable in the moderate-sample regime that erasure operates in (typically $n \approx 7000$ representations per setting in our experiments). We therefore estimate only the local tangent space $T_{\mathbf{x}_i}\mathcal{M}$ at each query point, using the local-PCA-of-secants construction of LTSA \citep{zhang-zha-2004-ltsa}. This local view is well matched to concept erasure for two reasons. First, every smooth manifold is locally Euclidean, so the tangent estimate is asymptotically exact in a sufficiently small neighborhood and the standard linear-algebraic operations we need --- gradient projection, trust-region step --- transfer without modification. Second, internal representations encode a concept along different directions in different regions of $\mathcal{M}$: the axis that distinguishes the concept in one neighborhood is generally not the axis that distinguishes it elsewhere. Training a fresh nonlinear scorer at each iteration and projecting its gradient onto the locally estimated tangent basis lets us extract the locally relevant concept direction wherever a sample lies, and remove only its on-manifold component.

\paragraph{Limitation of the local estimate, and the closed-form fix.}
A first-order tangent estimate is only accurate within a small neighborhood. This makes \TanGCE{} effective at removing the local nonlinear concept structure that nonlinear probes most readily exploit, but it is poorly suited to absorbing class-conditioned distributional moments that span the entire dataset --- in particular the mean shift between the two classes, and the variance asymmetry along axes where the class-conditional second moments differ. These global moments are linear, not local: a closed-form affine eraser on the full distribution removes them in a single step with provably minimum representation damage. We therefore precompose \TanGCE{} with two such closed-form preprocessing stages --- LEACE \citep{belrose-etal-2023-leace} for the first-moment alignment, and a rank-2 covariance-asymmetry projection (a rank-2 specialization of the $k$-LEACE family of \citealt{singh-ravfogel-2024-representation-surgery}) for the second-moment residual --- after which the iterative manifold loop refines what remains. We call these compositions \TanGCEp{} (LEACE only) and \TanGCEpp{} (LEACE $+$ rank-2 CovMatch; we use ``CovMatch'' to refer to this rank-2 closed-form step throughout), retaining bare \TanGCE{} (no preprocessing) as the variant that isolates the contribution of the manifold step. The closed-form stages are detailed in §\ref{sec:moment_preproc}; the iterative manifold loop is detailed in §\ref{sec:closed_form_lambda}--§\ref{sec:cov_tangce}.

\paragraph{Manifold-aware erasure as a complementary refinement.}
Consistent with MRH (§\ref{sec:mrh}), the manifold step composes with \emph{any} prior erasure technique: in §\ref{sec:tangce_on_baseline} we show that running \TanGCE{} on top of LEACE, LEACE\,+\,CovMatch, INLP, IGBP, or Obliviator further reduces nonlinear-probe leakage on every panel while preserving control accuracy. The gain is not specific to any one preprocessor, but to the fact that all of them leave behind the same locally curved residue that the manifold-constrained step removes.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{\TanGCEpp{}. Phase~1: LEACE + rank-2 covariance-asymmetry projection. Phase~2: iterative tangent-constrained step. Dropping line 1b yields \TanGCEp{}; dropping Phase 1 entirely yields \TanGCE{}.}
\label{alg:tangce}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\REQUIRE Representations $\mathbf{X}$, concept labels $\mathbf{y}$, $\#$rounds $T$, trust region $\varepsilon$, anisotropy $\alpha$, scorer hidden $h$.
\STATE \textbf{Phase~1 -- closed-form moment preprocessing} (Eqs.~\ref{eq:delta_sigma}--\ref{eq:covmatch_apply}):
\STATE \hspace{1em} \textbf{1a.} $\mathbf{X} \leftarrow \mathrm{LEACE}_{\mathbf{y}}(\mathbf{X})$ \hfill (rank-1 mean projection)
\STATE \hspace{1em} \textbf{1b.} $\mathbf{X} \leftarrow \mathbf{X} - \mathbf{D}\mathbf{D}^\top \mathbf{X}$, with $\mathbf{D}$ from Eq.~\ref{eq:covmatch_basis} \hfill (effective rank 2 post-LEACE)
\STATE \textbf{Phase~2 -- iterative tangent step} (Eqs.~\ref{eq:tangent_proj}--\ref{eq:tangent_erase_sigma}):
\FOR{$t = 1, \ldots, T$}
 \STATE Train a fresh 2-layer MLP scorer $f_t$ (hidden $h$) on the current $(\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{y})$.
 \STATE \textbf{For each row $\mathbf{x}_i$}: tangent-projected, spectrally-normalized trust-region step
 \STATE \hspace{2em} $\displaystyle \mathbf{x}_i \;\leftarrow\; \mathbf{x}_i - \lambda_i \sum_{k=1}^{r} \sigma_{i,k}^{\alpha} \, a_{i,k} \, \mathbf{v}_i^{(k)}, \quad \|\Delta\mathbf{x}_i\|_2 \leq \varepsilon \cdot s_i, \quad \alpha = 1.$
\ENDFOR
\STATE \textbf{return} edited $\mathbf{X}$.
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Phase~1: closed-form moment preprocessing}
\label{sec:moment_preproc}

Phase~1 is what turns \TanGCE{} into \TanGCEp{} (LEACE only) or \TanGCEpp{} (LEACE + rank-2 CovMatch). It exhausts 1st- and 2nd-moment concept signal in closed form so that the subsequent manifold-aware loop only has to attack the residual nonlinear pocket.

\paragraph{LEACE (1st moment).}
Fit a LEACE eraser \citep{belrose-etal-2023-leace} on $(\mathbf{X}_{\text{train}}, \mathbf{y})$ and apply to all splits. LEACE removes the rank-1 cross-covariance $\mathbf{\Sigma}_{xz}$, leaving no \emph{linear} classifier above majority-class accuracy with provably minimum representation damage among affine projections.

\paragraph{Covariance-asymmetry projection (2nd moment).}
LEACE matches class means but leaves the within-class asymmetry
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:delta_sigma}
\Delta\mathbf{\Sigma} = \mathbf{\Sigma}_{+} - \mathbf{\Sigma}_{-}.
\end{equation}
A nonlinear probe still recovers concept information from variance asymmetry along axes where $\mathbf{\Sigma}_{+}$ and $\mathbf{\Sigma}_{-}$ differ. We append a rank-$2$ projection: the top-$2$ eigenvectors of $\Delta\mathbf{\Sigma}$ ranked by $|\lambda|$, orthonormalized with a residual mean direction via QR,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:covmatch_basis}
\mathbf{D} = \mathrm{QR}\!\left([\,\hat{\mathbf{d}}_{\text{mean}} \;\big|\; \mathbf{e}_1, \mathbf{e}_2\,]\right) \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times 3},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:covmatch_apply}
\tilde{\mathbf{x}} = \mathbf{x} - \mathbf{D}\mathbf{D}^\top \mathbf{x}.
\end{equation}
Because LEACE has aligned class means, $\hat{\mathbf{d}}_{\text{mean}} \approx \mathbf{0}$ and the effective rank removed is two. This is a special case of the $k$-LEACE framework of \citet{singh-ravfogel-2024-representation-surgery} restricted to second-order eigenvectors, which are stable to estimate from $n \approx 7000$ examples while higher-order tensors are not. We fix $K{=}2$ across all 119 settings: for a binary concept, the top-2 eigenvectors of $\Delta\mathbf{\Sigma}$ carry the two axes of class-conditional variance asymmetry; subsequent eigenvectors describe noise rather than class-discriminative signal. Empirically, $K{\ge}3$ adds no leakage reduction and begins to erode control accuracy (see Tab.~\ref{tab:per_panel_breakdown}).

The two stages erase structurally distinct signal (mean shift vs.\ variance asymmetry) and together remove an effective rank of at most $3$, negligible relative to the ambient dimension $d$ across our settings ($d = 768$ for the CLIP ViT-B/32 image embedding, and $d \in [896, 5376]$ across the 13 LLM hidden sizes), exposing the residual nonlinear structure that Phase~2 attacks.

\subsection{Local tangent estimation}

We estimate $T_{\mathbf{x}_i}\mathcal{M}$ by local PCA/SVD of nearest-neighbor secants, following the local tangent-space approximation used in LTSA \citep{zhang-zha-2004-ltsa}. For each query $\mathbf{x}_i$, let $\mathcal{N}_k(\mathbf{x}_i)$ be its $k$ nearest neighbors. Stack the centered secants $\mathbf{s}_{j_m} = \mathbf{x}_{j_m} - \mathbf{x}_i$ row-wise into $\mathbf{S}_i \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times d}$ and take its SVD,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:secant_svd}
\mathbf{S}_i = \mathbf{U}_i \, \mathrm{diag}(\sigma_{i,1}, \ldots, \sigma_{i,k}) \, \mathbf{V}_i^\top.
\end{equation}
The right singular vectors $\mathbf{V}_i$ are the directions in which neighbors extend; the singular values $\sigma_{i,k}$ are how far they extend along each. The implied local covariance is $\mathbf{C}_i = \tfrac{1}{k}\mathbf{V}_i \, \mathrm{diag}(\sigma_{i,k}^2) \, \mathbf{V}_i^\top$. Truncating to the top $r$ right singular vectors gives the tangent basis
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_basis}
\mathbf{B}_i = [\mathbf{v}_i^{(1)}, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_i^{(r)}] \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times r},
\end{equation}
where the tangent rank $r$ is set automatically per setting by the TwoNN intrinsic-dimension estimator of \citet{facco-etal-2017-two-nn}. TwoNN reads off the local intrinsic dimension from the ratio of each point's distance to its nearest and second-nearest neighbor: under the assumption that the data lie on a $d^{*}$-dimensional manifold with locally near-uniform density, those ratios follow a Pareto distribution whose shape parameter equals $d^{*}$, and TwoNN returns the maximum-likelihood fit. We need this because the tangent rank is dataset and model-dependent, intrinsic dimension varies across LLM scales, families, concepts, and modalities, so a single fixed $r$ would over or under-truncate on most settings. We use TwoNN's per-setting estimate and floor it at $r \ge 8$ to guarantee enough degrees of freedom for the manifold-aware step. The tangent projector is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_proj}
\mathbf{P}_{T_i} = \mathbf{B}_i \mathbf{B}_i^\top.
\end{equation}
We treat the SVD basis as a first-order stand-in for $T_{\mathbf{x}_i}\mathcal{M}$, not as a guarantee of smooth-manifold structure.

\subsection{Tangent-constrained update}

The ambient unit gradient at $\mathbf{x}_i$ is $\mathbf{u}_i = \nabla f_t(\mathbf{x}_i) / \|\nabla f_t(\mathbf{x}_i)\|$. \TanGCE{} replaces it with its tangent projection,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_direction}
\mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}} = \mathbf{P}_{T_i} \mathbf{u}_i = \mathbf{B}_i (\mathbf{B}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_i),
\end{equation}
not re-normalized: $\|\mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}\| \le 1$ acts as an overlap weight that attenuates the update when the ambient gradient has little support in the estimated tangent space. The isotropic update is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_erase}
\tilde{\mathbf{x}}_i = \mathbf{x}_i - \lambda \, (\mathbf{x}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}) \, \mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}},
\end{equation}
with $\lambda$ a step-size scalar (replaced below by a closed-form per-sample $\lambda_i$). \AmbGCEpp{} is the direct ambient ablation (used on both NLP and Vision): same closed-form preprocessing, same iterative loop, same scorer, same $\lambda$, but applies the update in the full ambient space without the manifold projection. We retain Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase} as the conceptual entry point and as the $\alpha = 0$ ablation; the canonical \TanGCE{} update is the spectrally-normalized generalization in §\ref{sec:cov_tangce}.

\subsection{Anisotropic trust-region step in tangent SVD coordinates}
\label{sec:closed_form_lambda}
\label{sec:cov_tangce}

\paragraph{The simple update rule.}
The skeleton of every iteration is the tangent-projected gradient step of Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase}: subtract $\lambda \cdot (\mathbf{x}_i^\top \mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}) \mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}$ from $\mathbf{x}_i$, where $\mathbf{u}_i^{\mathrm{tan}}$ is the tangent-projected unit gradient and $\lambda$ is a step size. The remaining design questions are (i) how large $\lambda$ should be per sample and (ii) along which tangent axes the step should put its mass. We never literally take a $\lambda{=}1$ step in practice; we treat it only as the bookkeeping reference at which the displacement vector is unscaled.

\paragraph{Local trust region as a constraint optimisation.}
A uniform global $\lambda$ over-edits some samples while under-editing others. We instead solve, per sample, the one-dimensional constrained problem
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:trust_region}
\lambda_i \;=\; \arg\max_{\lambda \le \lambda_{\max}} \, \lambda
\quad \text{subject to} \quad
\| \tilde{\mathbf{x}}_i - \mathbf{x}_i \|_2 \le \varepsilon \cdot s_i,
\end{equation}
i.e.\ the largest $\lambda$ for which the displacement stays within a fraction $\varepsilon$ of a per-sample local scale $s_i$ (the precise definition of $s_i$ comes below). Letting $\mathbf{v}_i$ be the unscaled tangent-step direction (the displacement vector that Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase} would produce at the bookkeeping reference $\lambda{=}1$), the constraint $\|\lambda \, \mathbf{v}_i\|_2 \le \varepsilon \, s_i$ has the closed-form maximiser
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:lambda_closed_form}
\boxed{\;\;\lambda_i = \min\!\left( \lambda_{\max}, \; \frac{\varepsilon \cdot s_i}{\|\mathbf{v}_i\|} \right)\;\;}
\end{equation}
with $\lambda_{\max}=64$ a safety cap that bites only when $\|\mathbf{v}_i\| \to 0$. We use $\varepsilon = 0.1$ across every setting; $\lambda_i$ then takes whatever (typically large) value the constraint admits, sample by sample.

\paragraph{Tangent axes have different ``lengths''.}
The local linear approximation produced by the secant SVD (Eq.~\ref{eq:secant_svd}) decomposes the tangent space into right singular vectors $\{\mathbf{v}_i^{(k)}\}$ and singular values $\{\sigma_{i,k}\}$. The singular values are the per-axis ``lengths'' --- how far neighbors of $\mathbf{x}_i$ extend along each tangent direction. We will call the directions with large $\sigma_{i,k}$ the \emph{long axes} of the neighborhood (high-$\sigma$ for short) and the directions with small $\sigma_{i,k}$ the \emph{thin axes} (low-$\sigma$). A direction-agnostic Euclidean radius treats every axis identically: it over-allocates step budget along thin axes (where the linear approximation is fragile and a small step in coordinate space already escapes the manifold) and under-allocates along long axes (where neighbors give the linear approximation generous support).

\paragraph{Per-axis budget via spectral normalization.}
Respecting the per-axis lengths means measuring step magnitude in $\sigma$-normalised coordinates: divide each tangent coordinate of the move by the local singular value of its axis. This is Mahalanobis whitening expressed through the SVD, the local secant covariance is $\mathbf{C}_i = \tfrac{1}{k}\mathbf{V}_i \mathrm{diag}(\sigma_{i,k}^2)\mathbf{V}_i^\top$ and so $\mathbf{C}_i^{-1/2}$ acts diagonally as $\sigma_{i,k}^{-1}$ in the SVD basis, recovering the per-axis division for free. Concretely, we use the spectrally-normalised neighborhood radius
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:s_spectral}
s_i = R_i^{\mathrm{spec}} \cdot s_i^{\mathrm{maha}}, \qquad
R_i^{\mathrm{spec}} = \mathrm{median}_{j \in \mathcal{N}_k(i)} \, \|\mathbf{B}_i^\top (\mathbf{x}_j - \mathbf{x}_i) / \boldsymbol\sigma_i \|_2,
\end{equation}
with $\boldsymbol\sigma_i = (\sigma_{i,1}, \ldots, \sigma_{i,r})$ and $s_i^{\mathrm{maha}} = 1 / \|\boldsymbol{a}_i / (\boldsymbol\sigma_i + \delta)\|_2$ (component-wise), where $\boldsymbol{a}_i$ are the tangent coordinates of the displacement defined just below. $\varepsilon$ is then a fraction of the local spectrally-normalised neighborhood; setting $s_i = r_i$ (mean $k$-NN distance) recovers the Euclidean radial scale and is reported in App.\ as an ablation.

The spectrally-normalised step itself replaces Eq.~\ref{eq:tangent_erase} with
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:tangent_erase_sigma}
\boxed{\;\;
\tilde{\mathbf{x}}_i \;=\; \mathbf{x}_i \;-\; \lambda_i \sum_{k=1}^{r} \sigma_{i,k}^{\alpha} \, a_{i,k} \, \mathbf{v}_i^{(k)}
\;\;}
\end{equation}
where $a_{i,k} = \langle \mathbf{v}_i, \mathbf{v}_i^{(k)} \rangle$ are the SVD-basis coordinates of the unscaled tangent displacement and $\alpha = 1$ in the main experiments. Each tangent coefficient is now scaled by the local extent of the neighborhood along that axis: long-axis (high-$\sigma$) directions receive larger updates because the linear approximation is reliable there, and thin-axis (low-$\sigma$) directions are suppressed because moving along them most easily leaves the manifold.

\paragraph{Relation to local covariance and Mahalanobis scaling.}
The update can also be viewed as applying a positive spectral power of the local secant covariance. The empirical covariance of the centered secants has eigenvectors $\mathbf{v}_i^{(k)}$ and eigenvalues proportional to $\sigma_{i,k}^2$, so scaling each tangent coordinate by $\sigma_{i,k}$ corresponds, up to a global constant, to applying the positive square root of this local covariance ($\mathbf{C}_i^{1/2}$). This is the opposite of whitening: Mahalanobis normalisation downweights high-variance directions through an inverse covariance power, whereas we use a positive power to move preferentially along directions that are well supported by nearby representations. App.~\ref{sec:appendix_alpha_regimes} details the full $\sigma_{i,k}^{\alpha}$ family with $\alpha \in \{0, 1, 2\}$, corresponding respectively to the isotropic ablation, our default, and the natural-gradient / full-Mahalanobis trust-region optimum.

The trust-region magnitude in Eq.~\ref{eq:lambda_closed_form} is enforced after spectral normalisation (replacing $\|\mathbf{v}_i\|$ by the norm of the spectrally-normalised displacement in the denominator), so the actual ambient move still respects $\| \tilde{\mathbf{x}}_i - \mathbf{x}_i \|_2 \le \varepsilon \cdot s_i$. Anisotropy reshapes the \emph{direction} of the step, not its budget.

\subsection{Iterative loop and relation to baselines}

Each round trains a fresh nonlinear scorer on the current $(\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{y})$, recomputes per-sample gradients, and applies the manifold-aware update. The fresh-scorer loop follows \citet{elazar-goldberg-2018-adversarial}: success against any single trained classifier is not evidence the concept is gone. The iterative loop alone is \TanGCE{}; combined with LEACE preprocessing it is \TanGCEp{}; combined with LEACE + rank-2 CovMatch it is \TanGCEpp{}. \AmbGCEpp{} keeps the same closed-form preprocessing, the same loop and the same trust region, but applies the update in the full ambient space --- the direct ablation that isolates the manifold constraint. The two nonlinear baselines we compare against, IGBP \citep{iskander-etal-2023-shielded} and Obliviator \citep{akbari-etal-2025-obliviator}, share our supervision regime (target labels used during fitting, control concepts never seen by the eraser) and differ only in how the update is constructed: IGBP retracts toward an iteratively-retrained 2-class probe boundary; Obliviator solves an HSIC-penalised RKHS objective via random Fourier feature eigenvalue projection. \TanGCE{} differs from both by constraining each update to a locally estimated tangent space.
