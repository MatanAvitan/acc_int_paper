\section{Conclusion}

The completed paper runs already support a narrower, evidence-backed conclusion. Sycophancy is geometrically more nonlinear than Bias in Bios, and tangent-based erasure consistently achieves lower measured concept leakage than the completed ambient baselines on sycophancy, safety, and gender. That makes manifold-aware erasure a supported hypothesis for stronger concept removal in nonlinear settings.

At the same time, the current draft should stop short of claiming that tangent erasure already dominates the utility-fidelity tradeoff. In the finished runs, the lower-leakage tangent points typically require larger displacement and often incur equal or larger utility loss than ambient baselines. Appendix snapshot tables are directionally consistent with the same removal advantage at deeper layers, but the paper is strongest if it uses them as status evidence rather than as the basis for stronger causal or Pareto-frontier claims. The manuscript therefore lands best as support for the geometry contrast and for tangent's removal advantage, while reserving the broader utility-preservation claim for matched-leakage analyses and future replications.
