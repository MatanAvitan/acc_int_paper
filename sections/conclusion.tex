\section{Conclusion}

The completed paper runs already support a narrower, evidence-backed conclusion. Sycophancy is geometrically more nonlinear than Bias in Bios, and tangent-based erasure consistently achieves lower measured concept leakage than the completed ambient baselines on sycophancy, safety, and gender. That makes manifold-aware erasure a supported hypothesis for stronger concept removal in nonlinear settings.

At the same time, the current draft should stop short of claiming that tangent erasure already dominates the utility-fidelity tradeoff. In the finished runs, the lower-leakage tangent points typically require larger displacement and often incur equal or larger utility loss than ambient baselines. The paper is therefore strongest if it presents the current evidence as support for the geometry contrast and for tangent's removal advantage, while reserving the stronger utility-preservation claim for the still-running iterative sweeps and the planned matched-leakage analyses.
