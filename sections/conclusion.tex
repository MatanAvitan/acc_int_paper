\section{Conclusion}

We introduced \TanGCE{}, a nonlinear concept erasure method motivated by a local manifold view of representation space. Hidden states live in $\mathbb{R}^d$ but natural representations concentrate near a much smaller, structured support; tangent projection plus a local-$\sigma$ preconditioner restricts every erasure step to a fraction of the locally supported neighborhood. A two-stage method (closed-form LEACE + rank-2 covariance projection, then iterative tangent-constrained nonlinear erasure) operationalizes this view.

Across 119 settings under fixed method hyperparameters, \TanGCE{} reaches the per-setting majority-class probe-accuracy floor on more settings than the strongest published unsupervised baseline while damaging the control task less. The gain is largest on the most nonlinear concept (gender) and on the most-correlated CelebA control regime; both are regimes where ambient updates most readily push representations off-support. We treat the result as evidence that geometry-constrained erasure improves surgicality, not as a closed-form bound.

Several directions remain open. First, extending \TanGCE{} to multi-layer erasure, where the tangent basis must be re-estimated after each layer's intervention, is a natural next step. Second, our tangent estimator is a first-order PCA-based stand-in; richer local manifold estimators may yield tighter tangent bases on the settings where the SVD truncation is the bottleneck. Third, connecting tangent-estimation accuracy to erasure quality through a formal bound would replace the empirical evidence we report here with a guarantee.
