\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure aims to remove a target concept from a representation with minimal damage to the rest. Unconstrained ambient-space updates can distort unrelated concepts; we propose \TanGCE{}, a tangent-constrained method that restricts every erasure step to a local tangent approximation estimated by SVD of nearest-neighbor secants and reweights it by the local singular spectrum, so each axis is updated proportional to the manifold's local extent. A two-stage closed-form preprocessing (LEACE + rank-2 covariance-asymmetry projection) exhausts 1st- and 2nd-moment concept signal before the iterative tangent loop runs. We evaluate on 119 panels: 13 language models (0.5B--27B parameters) $\times$ 3 concepts (sycophancy, gender, safety) at 50\%-depth, plus 40 binary CelebA-CLIP attributes $\times$ 2 control-set regimes — all under the same fixed pipeline hyperparameters with no per-panel tuning. \TanGCE{} reaches the majority-class probe-accuracy floor at every control-damage budget on $26$ to $33$ of $39$ NLP panels (vs.\ $22$ to $26$ for the strongest published unsupervised baseline) while damaging the control task less, and is the only method that retains an admissible operating point on every CelebA attribute when target and controls are highly correlated.
