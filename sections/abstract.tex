\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure must remove a target concept without damaging the rest of the representation, yet unconstrained updates in the full representation space can cause unnecessary distortion and leave residual signal. We propose \TanGCE{}, a new manifold-aware erasure method that estimates local tangent spaces with neighborhood PCA and restricts erasure updates to that local support \ye{this sentence has a lot of jargon. tone it down} \ma{There's no argument to tone town here, it's simply a factual description of the method.}\ye{it's not simple as it's not clear what you do...}. We rely on the intuition that interventions should follow the same local support where natural representations lie: if representations concentrate near a low-dimensional neighborhood, effective edits should stay on that support rather than push them away. Across evaluations spanning 13 language models across five families (0.5B--27B parameters) and a cross-modal vision benchmark (CelebA with CLIP ViT-B/32), \TanGCE{} consistently removes more recoverable nonlinear concept signal than its ambient ablation and the prior baselines we compare against, while typically incurring less control damage than distortion-based methods.
