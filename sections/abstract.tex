\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure methods that operate in the ambient representation space can push representations off the data manifold, creating spurious correlations rather than removing information. We argue that both erasure and its evaluation should respect the intrinsic geometry of the representation manifold. On the erasure side, we introduce Tangent Gradient-based Concept Erasure (TanGCE), which constrains erasure gradients to the local tangent space estimated via neighborhood PCA. On the evaluation side, we adopt a $k$-nearest-neighbor mutual information estimator that measures concept information through local neighborhood structure---the same geometric lens that TanGCE uses for intervention. We complement this with unsupervised sparse autoencoder diagnostics that detect feature-level redistribution without requiring control-task labels. Across three concept domains---sycophancy, gender, and safety---in Qwen2.5-0.5B, and 40 facial attributes in CLIP ViT-B/32 representations of CelebA, TanGCE achieves the lowest nonlinear leakage while preserving control-task accuracy and maintaining near-zero feature redistribution. A $\lambda$ sweep reveals that ambient erasure at high step sizes \emph{increases} concept mutual information by laundering it into off-manifold displacement, a failure mode that TanGCE avoids by construction.
