\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure aims to remove a target concept from a representation with minimal damage to the rest of the representation, yet unconstrained updates in ambient space can distort unrelated concepts. We propose \TanGCE{}: a manifold-aware erasure method that estimates a local tangent space at each representation via the SVD of the secants to its nearest neighbors, and restricts every erasure step to lie inside that locally estimated subspace. The intuition is that if representations concentrate near a low-dimensional neighborhood, effective edits should stay on that local support rather than push them off it. To exhaust easy concept signal before the iterative loop runs, we precede it with two closed-form moment-matching projections that remove the rank-1 mean-shift between classes (LEACE) and the rank-2 covariance asymmetry between classes; the iterative tangent-constrained loop then erases the residual nonlinear concept signal. We evaluate at scale: 13 language models from five families (0.5B--27B parameters) on three concepts (sycophancy, gender, safety) at the 50\%-depth layer (39 NLP panels in total), plus a cross-modal vision benchmark on CelebA with CLIP ViT-B/32 covering all 40 binary facial attributes under two control regimes (5 least- and 5 most-correlated attributes per target, 80 vision panels in total) — four datasets and 119 evaluation panels overall, all under the same fixed pipeline hyperparameters (no per-panel tuning). The full pipeline strictly beats the strongest published unsupervised baseline (Obliviator) at every control-damage budget on the 39-panel language-model grid, reaching the majority-class floor on $23/39$ panels (vs. $14/39$ for Obliviator).
