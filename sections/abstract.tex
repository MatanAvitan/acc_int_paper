\section*{Abstract}

Hidden states live in a high-dimensional ambient space, but the representations used by natural inputs need not. We show that this geometric mismatch matters for nonlinear concept erasure. When representations concentrate near a locally low-dimensional manifold, ambient updates can move in directions that are poorly aligned with the geometry actually used by the model, while the relevant concept direction itself can vary across neighborhoods. This makes a single global erasure direction inadequate for the nonlinear setting. We address this with \TanGCE{}, which estimates local tangent spaces with neighborhood PCA and projects erasure updates onto that first-order local support. Across our completed text and vision benchmarks, \TanGCE{} achieves the strongest nonlinear erasure, outperforming all prior methods, including its ambient ablation \AmbGCE{}, under retrained probe evaluation. The broader implication is that manifold-aware editing is a promising principle not only for concept removal, but for representation editing and optimization more generally.
