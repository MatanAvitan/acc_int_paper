\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure is useful only when it removes a target attribute without destroying the other information encoded in the representation. This is hard in the nonlinear setting: information erased for one probe may remain recoverable by a fresh nonlinear probe. Direct gradient-based edits introduce a second failure mode: they may remove target signal by moving representations away from the region occupied by natural inputs, unintentionally damaging other encoded concepts. We propose \TanGCE{}, a geometry-constrained method for nonlinear concept erasure that uses each sample's local representation geometry to make the erasure surgical. \TanGCE{} first removes dominant low-order target signal with closed-form projections that eliminate the main mean and covariance differences between the target classes. It then targets the remaining nonlinear signal locally. For each representation, nearest-neighbor differences define tangent-like directions in which nearby representations actually vary; \TanGCE{} projects the concept-scorer gradient onto these directions, scales each coordinate by the neighborhood's local extent, and caps the update with a per-sample trust region. Thus, the erasure step is not merely nonlinear, but constrained to directions and scales supported by the local geometry of natural representations. We evaluate \TanGCE{} on 13 language models, three NLP concepts, and 40 CelebA-CLIP attributes under two control regimes, using one fixed set of hyperparameters with no per-setting tuning. Compared at the same allowed drop in control-concept accuracy, \TanGCE{} removes more target signal than the strongest published baseline. On the NLP settings, it drives a fresh nonlinear probe to the majority-class floor in $26$--$33$ settings, $13$--$20$ more than the baseline, while causing smaller control degradation. The advantage is largest when target and control attributes are entangled: on CelebA-CLIP, under highly correlated controls, \TanGCE{} is the only method that stays within the allowed control-accuracy drop for every target attribute.
