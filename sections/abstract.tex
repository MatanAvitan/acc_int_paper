\section*{Abstract}

Nonlinear concept erasure is hard because linear methods miss recoverable signal, while ambient-space edits ignore the geometry that natural representations actually use. We show that hidden states concentrate near local manifolds, so the right erasure directions are tangent directions, not arbitrary ambient directions. \TanGCE{} estimates each sample's local tangent space with neighborhood PCA and projects erasure updates onto that first-order support. Across text and vision benchmarks, \TanGCE{} achieves the strongest nonlinear erasure, with the lowest retrained probe accuracy, beating every prior method and consistently outperforming \AmbGCE{}. Our geometry analyses explain why: concept directions vary across neighborhoods, and a single global direction fails to capture this local structure. Manifold-aware editing therefore extends beyond concept removal, offering a general principle for representation editing and optimization in high-dimensional models.
