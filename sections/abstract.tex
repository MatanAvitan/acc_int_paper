\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure in language models often degrades model utility because erasure directions in high-dimensional ambient space frequently point orthogonal to the data manifold. These off-manifold edits displace representations into low-density regions, driving performance collapse. We present Gradient-based Concept Erasure with Tangent projection (\texttt{gce\_tangent}), which constrains erasure gradients to the local tangent space of the representation manifold. Our evaluation compares \texttt{gce\_tangent} against \texttt{gce\_ambient} on Qwen2.5-0.5B, using sycophancy as the primary setting and Bias in Bios (gender) as a diagnostic baseline. Results show that \texttt{gce\_tangent} achieves more effective concept removal with significantly lower utility degradation. Off-manifold displacement is the primary mechanism driving utility loss, establishing manifold-aware interventions as a requirement for surgical and effective concept erasure.
