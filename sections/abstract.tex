\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure in language models can degrade model utility. One plausible mechanism is that erasure directions computed in high-dimensional ambient space can point orthogonal to the data manifold, producing off-manifold edits that displace representations into low-density regions. We present Gradient-based Concept Erasure with Tangent projection (\texttt{gce\_tangent}), which constrains erasure gradients to the local tangent space of the representation manifold. On Qwen2.5-0.5B, the completed paper runs on sycophancy, safety, and Bias in Bios consistently show lower measured leakage for \texttt{gce\_tangent} than for \texttt{gce\_ambient}. The current evidence is narrower than our original thesis, however: the lower-leakage tangent settings often incur larger displacement and mixed utility retention, so the completed results support stronger removal but not yet a uniformly better leakage--utility frontier. Geometry diagnostics still strengthen the mechanism story by showing that sycophancy is substantially more nonlinear than gender, making manifold-aware erasure a promising direction for surgical concept removal.
