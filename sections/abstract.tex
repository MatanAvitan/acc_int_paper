\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure aims to remove target information from a representation without destroying other information it carries. This is difficult for nonlinear concepts: removing the signal used by one probe does not prevent a fresh nonlinear probe from recovering the concept, while unconstrained gradient-based edits can move representations away from the support of natural inputs. We introduce \TanGCE{}, a manifold-aware erasure method that constrains nonlinear edits to local tangent directions. For each representation, \TanGCE{} estimates a tangent basis from nearest-neighbor secants, projects the concept-scorer gradient onto this basis, and scales the resulting step by the local singular spectrum, so motion along each tangent axis is proportional to the neighborhood's extent along that axis. Before the iterative tangent loop, we remove dominant low-order target signal with closed-form LEACE and a rank-2 covariance-asymmetry projection. Across 119 settings, covering 13 language models, three NLP concepts, and 40 CelebA-CLIP attributes under two control regimes, \TanGCE{} uses one fixed set of hyperparameters with no per-setting tuning. On the 39 NLP settings, it reaches the majority-class probe-accuracy floor on $26$--$33$ settings across control-damage budgets, compared with $22$--$26$ for Obliviator, while preserving control accuracy better. On CelebA-CLIP, it is the only method that retains an admissible operating point for every attribute in the highly correlated control regime.
