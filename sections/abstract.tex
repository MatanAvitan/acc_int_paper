\section*{Abstract}

Although the ambient representation space of a language model is high-dimensional, the set of sentence representations used in practice can lie on a much lower-dimensional natural manifold. This creates a problem for gradient-based concept erasure: when erasure updates are computed in the full ambient space, they are likely to push representations off that manifold and damage control concepts instead of removing only the target one. We introduce Tangent Gradient-based Concept Erasure (TanGCE), which constrains nonlinear erasure updates to a locally estimated tangent space so that the intervention stays aligned with the geometry actually used by the model. This geometric constraint lets TanGCE safely employ larger erasure strengths $\lambda$ than ambient updates, improving removal of nonlinear concept information while keeping control-task changes small. Across three text datasets---sycophancy, gender, and safety---in Qwen2.5-0.5B, and a large vision benchmark with 40 facial attributes in CLIP ViT-B/32 representations of CelebA, TanGCE achieves the strongest erasure precision together with competitive control-concept preservation. These results support a simple picture: once concept removal is constrained to the natural manifold of representations, stronger interventions become possible without paying the same collateral-damage cost.
