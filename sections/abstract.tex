\section*{Abstract}

Concept erasure is useful only when it removes a target attribute without destroying the other information encoded in the representation. This is hard in the nonlinear setting: information erased for one probe may remain recoverable by a fresh nonlinear probe. Direct gradient-based edits introduce a second failure mode: they may remove target signal by pushing representations \emph{off the manifold} of natural representations, damaging the many other concepts encoded along that manifold --- most of which are unlabeled and cannot be explicitly protected as controls. Our central argument is that nonlinear erasure becomes surgical precisely when each edit is constrained to stay on the natural-representation manifold: an on-manifold step removes target signal in directions the representation itself supports, so collateral damage to unknown, unlabeled control concepts is bounded by construction. We propose the \TanGCE{} family, a manifold-aware approach to nonlinear concept erasure that operationalises this argument. The core method, \TanGCE{}, is an iterative manifold-constrained step: for each representation, nearest-neighbor differences estimate the local tangent space of the manifold, and a fresh nonlinear scorer's gradient is projected onto that tangent, scaled by the local manifold extent, and capped by a per-sample trust region. Two preprocessing-augmented variants compose this core with closed-form linear projections: \TanGCEp{} prepends LEACE (mean alignment between target classes), and \TanGCEpp{} additionally prepends a rank-2 covariance projection that exhausts the second-moment target signal. We evaluate the family on 13 language models, three NLP concepts, and 40 CelebA-CLIP attributes under two control regimes, using one fixed set of hyperparameters with no per-setting tuning. Compared at the same allowed drop in control-concept accuracy, \TanGCEpp{} removes more target signal than the strongest published baseline. On the NLP settings, it drives a fresh nonlinear probe to the majority-class chance level in $26$--$33$ settings, $13$--$20$ more than the baseline, while causing smaller control degradation. The advantage is largest when target and control attributes are entangled: on CelebA-CLIP, under highly correlated controls, \TanGCEp{} is the only method that stays within the allowed control-accuracy drop for every target attribute --- the empirical signature of an erasure step that respects the manifold the controls also live on.
